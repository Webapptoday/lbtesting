The Core Question

What This Site Is About

One focused question, studied in depth: can a routine blood draw reliably detect cancer early enough to change outcomes?

◔

What Is Liquid Biopsy?

A blood test that detects circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA), tumor cells, and molecular biomarkers shed by cancers into the bloodstream, without any invasive tissue sampling.

Learn the biology →
⚠

Why It’s Hard

Early-stage tumors shed vanishingly rare signals: sometimes one mutant DNA fragment among a billion normal ones. Sensitivity and specificity trade-offs remain unsolved for most cancer types at asymptomatic stages.

Read the analysis →
▶

Where It Works Now

Current clinical utility is clearest in treatment monitoring, minimal residual disease detection post-surgery, and therapy resistance tracking, not broad population screening.

See the evidence →
The Process

How Liquid Biopsy Works

01
Blood Draw

Patient provides a standard venipuncture sample. No surgery, no sedation.

02
Biomarker Isolation

ctDNA, CTCs, and exosomes are separated from plasma or serum.

03
Molecular Analysis

NGS, digital PCR, or methylation sequencing identifies cancer-specific signatures.

04
Clinical Interpretation

Results inform diagnosis, treatment response, or recurrence monitoring decisions.

“Liquid biopsy represents a paradigm shift toward precision oncology, but clinical utility depends on solving the fundamental challenge of detecting rare circulating biomarkers in disease-free patients. This is a sensitivity and specificity problem that remains unsolved for most cancer types at truly early stages.”
