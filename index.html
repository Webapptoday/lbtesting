Understanding Liquid Biopsy

What is Liquid Biopsy?

A non-invasive blood test that detects circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA), circulating tumor cells (CTCs), and other cancer biomarkers released into the bloodstream.

Learn more →

Why Early Detection Matters

Cancer detected at earlier stages generally has better treatment outcomes, higher survival rates, and more treatment options available.

Learn more →

Scientific Limitations

Current challenges include low biomarker abundance in early disease, sensitivity/specificity trade-offs, and barriers to clinical validation and adoption.

Learn more →

How Liquid Biopsy Works

Sample Collection

Patient provides blood sample via simple venipuncture

Biomarker Isolation

ctDNA, CTCs, and exosomes are extracted from plasma or serum

Detection & Analysis

Molecular techniques (PCR, sequencing, etc.) identify cancer-specific signatures

Clinical Interpretation

Results inform diagnosis, prognosis, or monitoring decisions

Key Insight

"Liquid biopsy represents a paradigm shift toward precision oncology, but clinical utility depends on solving the fundamental challenge of detecting rare circulating biomarkers in disease-free patients—a sensitivity and specificity problem that remains unsolved for most cancer types at truly early stages."
